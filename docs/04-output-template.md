# Final Output Template

The Synthesizer produces a **short** breakdown — skimmable in under two minutes.
Warm, specific, and framed as experiments to try. The structure below is the target;
the Editor trims anything low-confidence or repetitive.

---

## Layout

```
🎯 THE ONE-LINER
   A single sentence capturing the person's proportional "signature" and the
   single highest-leverage styling idea. (This is the takeaway if they read nothing else.)

📐 WHAT THE PHOTOS SHOW  (facts, lightly held)
   • Proportions: 3–4 bullets — vertical balance, waist placement, shoulder/hip read.
   • Body-shape read: "closest to ___" + one sentence why. Confidence noted.
   • Coloring: undertone lean + contrast level, with the lighting caveat.

✨ PLAY WITH THESE  (the fun part — 3 to 5 ideas)
   Each idea = a concrete move + the principle behind it + a why-it-works line.
   e.g. "Higher-rise bottoms + tucked-in tops → lengthens the leg line because it
   resets where the eye thinks your waist is."

🔧 STYLES TO TWEAK, NOT COPY
   1–3 popular looks the person might be drawn to, with the specific adjustment that
   makes each one work *for them* instead of against them.
   e.g. "Oversized everything is having a moment — but head-to-toe volume can swallow
   your frame. Keep one axis fitted (usually the one you want to define)."

🧭 PRINCIPLES TO KEEP IN YOUR POCKET
   2–3 durable, portable rules of thumb that outlast any single outfit.

💬 CLOSING NOTE
   One encouraging line. Reiterate: food for thought, not rules. Your taste wins.
```

## Worked micro-example (illustrative tone, not real output)

> **🎯 The one-liner:** You've got a long torso and an even shoulder-to-hip balance —
> so your biggest lever is *defining a waistline and shortening the visual torso* to
> unlock some leg length.
>
> **📐 What the photos show**
> - Vertical: torso reads slightly longer than legs; natural waist sits a touch low. *(medium confidence — front + side agree)*
> - Balance: shoulders and hips look roughly even; waist is gently defined → reads **rectangle-leaning**. *(medium)*
> - Coloring: cooler undertone lean, medium contrast — but the lighting is warm, so treat this as a hunch. *(low)*
>
> **✨ Play with these**
> - **Define the middle:** belts at the natural waist, wrap styles, or a tucked-in top. *Principle: creating a waist adds shape to a balanced frame.*
> - **Shorten the torso, lengthen the leg:** higher-rise bottoms, cropped jackets that end at the hip bone. *Principle: waistline placement redistributes torso:leg ratio.*
> - **Vertical lines up top:** open collars, long necklaces, V- and scoop necks draw the eye up and narrow the torso. *Principle: vertical lines elongate.*
>
> **🔧 Styles to tweak, not copy**
> - *Low-rise + long untucked top* (very trendy): on a long torso this stacks length on length. If you love it, tuck a front-corner or add a belt to reclaim the waist.
>
> **🧭 Pocket principles**
> - When in doubt, put the fitted piece where you want the eye to land.
> - One point of emphasis per outfit beats three competing ones.
>
> **💬** All of this is a starting point to play with — your eye and what makes you
> feel good are the real experts here.

## Editorial rules the Synthesizer enforces

- **Length:** aim for ~250–400 words in the final render. Cut before you pad.
- **Specificity:** every suggestion names a garment, cut, or move — never just "wear
  flattering clothes."
- **Traceability:** each "play with these" item maps to a principle ID internally
  (not shown to the user, but checked).
- **Confidence honesty:** anything the analysts marked `low` is either dropped or
  explicitly hedged. Never launder a guess into a fact.
- **Tone check:** no "flaws," "hide," "problem areas," "slimming as duty." Reframe to
  "define / balance / draw the eye / lengthen / play up."
- **No overload:** max 5 "play with" ideas, max 3 trend tweaks, max 3 pocket
  principles. More than that and nothing sticks.
